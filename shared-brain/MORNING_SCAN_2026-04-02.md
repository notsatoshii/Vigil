## CONTRARIAN VIEW

The prediction market sector is showing signs of classic hype-cycle dynamics:

- Valuations ($22B Kalshi, $20B Polymarket) are 10-20x revenue multiples based on current volume trajectories. If the Iran war resolves and the US midterms produce an early blowout, volume could fall 40-60% from current peaks.
- Congressional pressure is bipartisan now (Schiff and Curtis both sponsoring the "Gambling Act"). Republican control is protective in the near term, but any power shift in November midterms could accelerate unfavorable legislation.
- Insider trading probes could become politically embarrassing. The $400K Venezuela bet was not illegal, but the optics are bad. One high-profile criminal prosecution could trigger a media cycle that suppresses user growth for 12-18 months.
- DraftKings entering all 50 states with a full-featured app competes for casual users in a way that Polymarket and Kalshi cannot match (brand recognition, existing user base of 10M+).
- For LEVER specifically: being DeFi-native is an advantage only if DeFi users exist at scale on Base. Base TVL at $4B with 500K DAA is large by DeFi standards but tiny compared to Kalshi's 2M+ users.

The bull case is real and the sector is genuinely growing. But the "prediction markets are the next Bloomberg" narrative (from Paradigm) is getting ahead of the fundamentals. LEVER should position for the durable infrastructure layer, not the speculative volume peaks.
